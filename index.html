Frequently Asked Questions

What Are Common Challenges in IVF Treatment?

+

IVF treats various reasons of infertility like males and females being infertile, women having blocked fallopian tubes, men being low on sperm, women suffering from ovulatory disorders, and endometriosis amongst other factors. IVF is beneficial in circumstances such as advancing a family where there is a risk of genetic troubles by screening embryos for any defects before transferring.

When Is the Best Time to Consult an IVF Specialist?

+

You are advised to see an IVF expert if you and your partner are able to conceive in a span of a year or more. Women who are aged more than 35 years and have been unable to conceive in a span of 6 months are also advised to see an expert. Also, if you are suffering from underlying conditions such as PCOS, endometriosis, or male factors then it is a matter of time and you should consult an expert to increase your chances of conception.

What Are the Other Fertility Treatments Available Alongside IVF?

+

Other than IVF, other treatment options available are Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection also known as ICSI, intrauterine insemination also known as IUI, egg freezing, and also sperm making. Spinning IVF together with other options such as Genetic Screening Test for PGS couples is possible for children who happen to possess specific genetic disorders.

How Can Fertility Disorders Be Treated Without Martin Surgery?

+

Stimulating treatments for fertility disorders include hormonal treatment for ovulation stimulation, drugs such as Clomid to stimulate ovulation, or even changing one’s lifestyle by reducing the stressful situations, controlling their weight or even changing their diet to improve the chances of conception. Moreover, acupuncture treatment and herbal therapies are sometimes perceived as adjunct treatments.

What Does the Term ‘Minimally Invasive’ Encompass in IVF Processes?

+

The use of minimally invasive processes encompass methods in ART that aim to alleviate physical trauma and recovery time associated with the process. For example, the retrieval of oocytes is often accomplished through a small incision under the guidance of ultrasound and therefore recovery time is minimal. Likewise, the transfer of embryos is a treatment that is done without any form of surgery, thus reducing the level of discomfort and risks that the patient is exposed to.

Is There Any Risk Involved In Getting An IVF Treatment?

+

Like every medical procedure, seeking an IVF treatment does have some risks, the likes of OHSS, ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome, multiple pregnancies, and complications from egg retrieval. But all these risks can be addressed by supervision and case by case treatment by experienced experts.

An IVF cycle involves several procedures that need to be done in a certain order, they include firstly hormone therapy meant to stimulate and enhance the functionality of the ovaries, then blood tests and Ultrasounds, and consults. Can the Use of IVF Techniques Be Avoided?

+

Couples are most often dependent on IVF because of their fertility issues, and the use of IVF is certainly not preventable. But in some cases, it is possible to prevent the need for IVF by seeking timely treatment for infertility disorders, doing regular health checks, adopting a healthy lifestyle, and treating certain health issues such as PCOS or male fertility.
